Physics Handouts

This page has all the notes I think deserve a better position and reference. All these notes were written as a way to explore any problem or a idea at a more organic way to the reader. None of it is completely fault-free, feel free to give suggestions, cusses and cat pictures.

Great Handouts

  • Tensor and Differential Geometry Describes the mathematics of coordiante systems, transformation and I will work on it when I am confident with Curved Spacetime and Field Equations. It’s a lot of pre-study. It’s cool because all diagrams are near exact drawn using TiKZ Language.

  • Problem Journal (8 Jan, 2022) A journal I’ve worked on for over a year, has 115 problems so far (115 out of thousands I’ve attempted) with somewhat nicely written solutions. I am really happy about it because it is the outcome of all the things I’ve learned from writing the handouts featured below.

Mechanics

  • Poorman’s Introduction to Lagrangian Method Analytical Mechanics is a fantastic way to solve physical system’s Equation of Motion (simply said, acceleration). $${ \mathrm{d} \over \mathrm{d} t} { \partial L \over \partial \dot{x}} ={\partial L \over \partial x}$$ By this I introduce a straightforward approach to get started using the equation to solve problems. A more in depth understanding can be got from Ashmit Dutta’s handout, which is relatively demanding, here’s the link in physoly

  • Difficult Harmonic Oscillations Mathematical solutions to various “Difficult” Harmonics like Forced and Damped oscillations and other general cases using the idea of a General and Particular solution - neat and nice. $$ \ddot{x} + \omega^2 x = 0$$ The paper will look at the general case, $$ \ddot{x} + 2\gamma \dot{x} + \omega^2 x =0 $$ Sometimes while finding modes of oscillations the matrix method is helpful, here is a quick example.

  • Center of Mass derivation from Torque balance Written as a part of destructively critisizing my friends mechanics tutoring online. $$ \sum_{i = 1}^{N} \vec{\tau_i} = 0$$ That’s the main equation, where the torque caused by all the point masses is zeroed out. We need to find the position where it happens.

  • Ellipse from Random things It’s basically a 28 page low level research, I studied some phenomenons that yield ellipses and wrote about them here. More casual tone and informal. Starts from basic ways of drawing ellipse to the Lenz-Runge Vector. I ran into many troubles, this is one of the roadblocks.

  • Mechanics Camp 1,, 2, 3 3 beamer I used to take the classes in Derivation Scientific Society MechMania Mechanics Camp classes. These beamers are 80-ish page long with frame snaps, and was possible becasue of Vim-Snippets and some other python codes. Looking at it now I think it was too much but I enjoyed doing it though.

  • General Methods (bn) I can’t explain how long it took me to type this in Bengali. Inspired by Belikov’s book I tried writing a General Physics problem solution note, and I’ve worked on this for countless hours. This 20 page article was typed word by word in Bengali. But interesting nobody except me has read this. I would love to hear from you if you do.

  • Infant’s Calculus A 60 page introduction to Differentiation and the Integration part was discontinued. I did this to gain attention from Masruk vai and I did but get it. I discontinued the Integration part because the core problem beginners including me face/faced is the idea of approximation. Usually math texts start talking about limits, but I don’t like that approach so I used approximation of binomials as an aid. Thanks to “Fool’s Guide to Calculus Book” that finally solved some of my year long confusions.

  • Sidereal Time I wrote my own derivation to sidereal time using angular velocity when I didn’t understand what the other books were saying.

  • Irodov 1.138 Mechanics What’s different about this ordinary solution note is it has 2 solutions to the same problem, using two different chain of thoughts. It is a good note to guide you how to think about a problem if you are a beginner. The 5 page note basically is my thought process when I solved it.

  • Jaan Kalda’s Kinematics Translation (bn) It took a lot of effort to write this, and as I was in the middle I realized it was totally useless when I heard bdpho senior generation believes these problems are not core to making it to APHO/IPHO. I still don’t understand that idea but it was enough to stop my work that time because this translation won’t be worthwhile. But the takeaway was I had the opportunity to use the raw LaTeX code of Jaan Kalda, it also introduced me to he idea of using custom packages (sty files) and fonts.

Introductory Physics

More easy going and general kind off.

  • Binding Energy of Planet Basic derivation, very short.

  • Escape Velocity Written just to curb my enthusiasm, nothing great. It’s one of the earliest physics concept I understood myself so it worth mentioning.

It did make me sad and I wanted to do something if someone from a Class 7/8 level had the same issue with maths, hence this note - unfinished because I had to work on another book-sized note.
