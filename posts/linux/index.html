Linux

History

The first time I was exposed to Linux, an Operating System, was when I googled ways to draw inkscape diagrams very quickly to take notes in real time.

I did hit upon a gem. The simple website where Gilles Castel wrote articles on how he used LaTeX to write math in real time in the class to take notes was indeed a discovery; accompanied by Inkscape.

Castel’s bspwm with nord adornment.

I was more than impressed to look at the desktop interface, I though Linux was very cool, he added what he was using, the jargons “Ubuntu”, “bspwm”, “zathura”.

My first few attempts of being cool like him was trying to install Zathura in my Windows 10 machine. I still laugh at myself what I did.

Long story short, one day my windows updated and went miserably slow. I was extremely afraid to change a whole operating system on the only Laptop I may ever have custody of.

I did it anyway. The first installation of Ubuntu from the USB stick was a complete failure, and the second installation was complete but became a mess. It was 7 in evening when I started, when I was doing the second installation it was 2 at night, shivering of cold on my dining table, mosquito’s biting every possible inch of my arms and feet.

Now the pain wasn’t enough, the screen of the computer as it booted went black and it wrote that horrible piece of text sentence, which freaked my out.

>_ GRUB Rescue

The “Grand Unified Bootloader” was lost. It was pain, I didn’t know anything how OS works, and here’s this showing GRUB is not found.

I tried solving it, and the mosquitos continued biting. It was 3 at night when I live booted from the USB stick and finally a solution worked.

I finally had Linux in my system, Ubuntu. Looking back that experience had grown my curiousity and will to understand this system. Although I never felt the need to tinker with computers, using Linux and learning about it has been the way I spend my boring days in Quarantine for a year, straight.

One thing I love to do is finding extremely quick ways of doing things I need to do regularly. I like to Write Physics handouts and texts in LaTeX, and I wanted to do very efficiently, I like to use the word “ergonomic” to describe it.

Hence I learned doing typing, gradually increasing from 30 words per minute to 100. I installed snippets, so I can short cut to write math equations, used bspwm, which is a very minimal desktop environment that makes the windows’s tiled.

I loved every part of installing these and configuring their source code to fit my needs, it’s a thing that I love about Linux, you can make things just the way you want and know about it.

I am a type of guy who’d go to a road side food truck and ask the owner “may I make scrambled eggs in your pan?” (I actually do this XD). Linux was a complete fit.

My first system: Ubuntu 20.04 LTS

Initially I didn’t know anything about it, but gradually I understood things. It was painful to go through the software github pages, but gradually I got the essense. Installing a status bar with bspwm (the window manager) called Polybar took me intense 3 days of sleep deprivation to get that working.

I’d say nobody should be wasting their time like that, but at end I had a better view of my system and understood how the nook and cranny works.

I was pretty happy with what I set up.

I am making this site from Fedora Linux, but I am now assured I want to be in Debian based systems. I will soon do my final landing on Ubuntu after I use a OpenSuse, Manjaro and Debian multiboot for a month.
